# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, one surface. **Recruiters and hiring managers** land cold, skim fast, need to leave impressed. **Developer peers and collaborators** come curious, want to poke around, appreciate craft and inside jokes. Both must be served without compromise: the site can't condescend to recruiters or bore developers.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio with a gamified play-money economy threaded through the whole site. The earnings system (balances, purchasable color themes, quests, payouts) is the differentiator — it turns passive browsing into light exploration and signals that the owner can build unusual, delightful things. Success means a recruiter forms a strong first impression AND a developer actually plays with it.

## Brand Personality

Playful · Sharp · Confident. Wit-forward without being tryhard. The gamification is a real design choice, not a gimmick. Confident enough to have opinions; sharp enough to execute them cleanly.

## Anti-references

Sterile SaaS landing pages: blue CTAs, stock photography, feature grids, zero personality. Also: cookie-cutter dark-mode dev portfolios with terminal fonts and "Hello, World" hero text.

## Design Principles

1. **Delight is structural, not decorative.** The gamification earns its presence by being genuinely interactive — not a skin. Any design choice should reinforce participation, not just look good.
2. **Dual legibility.** Every surface must read quickly for a 10-second recruiter skim AND reward slow exploration for a developer. Nothing should require explanation to impress; nothing should be exhausted after one look.
3. **Confidence over safety.** Avoid choices that hedge. If there's a bolder, more opinionated version of something, default there. Sterile is the failure mode.
4. **Economy as narrative.** The balance, themes, and quests aren't easter eggs — they're the story. UI decisions should make the economy feel real and worth engaging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA. Reduced-motion support already wired via `useReducedMotion`. Color themes vary — selected-state affordances must not rely on color alone (shape, scale, or position must also differ).
